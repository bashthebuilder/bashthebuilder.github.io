--- layout: archive permalink: /year-archive/ title: "Prospective Research Student Information" author_profile: true redirect_from: - /wordpress/blog-posts/ --- Please read the content mentioned below carefully before contacting me for a possible supervision request.
  1. Are you accepting PhD students?
    2.  Yes, I am. Now I am a bit careful before accepting one given I already have a big team to handle including my research.
  2. How can I work with you?
    3.
    1. Check my profile to identify if your research interests closely align with mine.
    2. Send me your CV and a short research proposal.
    3. Wait for my response.
    4. If you do not receive my repsonse within a week, perhaps you have randomly sent me your documents without even checking my profile.
  3. Can I still apply without contacting you first?
    4.  Yes, you can. However, if you want to work with me, please mention my name as your potential supervisor in your application. You can apply here . I will carefully review your application and a decision will follow regarding further steps.
  4. What are my minimum entry requirements?
    5. While I work mainly with text data, my main speciality lies in Mathematics. I try and propose new methods to model text data. Therefore, I would prefer someone who has a strong grounding in Mathematics, Statistics, Physics, or related subjects. If you have a similar passion, you are the right person to work with me. However, over the years, I have realised that not everyone is alike. Some students who are not very strong initially quickly catch-up with the challenges and do exceedingly well. This is why I tend to not directly reject applications if I see weak Mathematical skills. I try to understand the applicant from various perspectives first, interview the applicant to understand the individual even better so that I could make an informed decision.
  5. Do you accept short-term interns?
    6.  Usually, I do not. Given what COVID-19 has taught us, I can discuss a possible remote "unpaid" internship setup with you where you work on research problems in your campus and I can remotely supervise you. If you do a good job, I will not hesitate to write a letter of recommedation for you.
  6. Do you accept visiting students?
    7.  Yes, I do. Please feel free to contact me regarding this further. It all depends whether I have set aside some funds for visiting scholars.
